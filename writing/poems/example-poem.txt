The light comes in the same
as always, a pale thing
leaning through the window
without asking.

I have made a place for it
on the floor. It stays
an hour, then moves on,
as light does.

I think of how it traveled
ninety-three million miles
to warm this particular
square of wood,

and I think that is
something—that it came
this far and found
a place to rest.
