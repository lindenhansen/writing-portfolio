Magical cowboy,
I finally saw your handwriting at the
Mongolian calligraphy institute.

In a knapsack you packed
Sweaters that hold themselves up,
And
Little teacups
From on the lake
Under the
Lattice.

At a tea ceremony where you did not
Attend
But would have killed—
Yes, killed—
To be,
I held the dragon in my hands
Without quick breath
And a strong heart.

Shot through its eye a princely blue light—
Quiet whispers,
Morning golden,
Birds tweaking,
Conspire at once
To rustle the breeze.

Shimmers in the pot echo
Adventures stolen.

Measured in
Minutes in the chair.

Silence held down
The highway.

Big bear hugs.

Slowly making chips in
The granite.

Fine,
Smooth talker,
put a pin in me.

The strings that tie—
They are
swollen
With a good
And true
And—

Right here is where
I wanna be.

Milling about the afterlife,
Which you do not believe in.
