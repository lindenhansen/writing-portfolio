How I miss grass and mountain soil
that when you touch it it breathes.
It feels. There is warmth in the earth
and a breeze nudges the air. Yellow
stamped directly on my skin. A field
so long I could sprint for many minutes
and still not reach the end. A stream
so clear rocks ripple in shape and size
at the bottom—I'm staring straight
through it. A summer day that feels
like a Mazzy Star song. My home in
the trees. Rocky crags that pierce the
sky. A Wild West unnamed still.
Chiselers running in a Gator's wake.
Rumbly hum on the dirt road. And
all this again baked in arid dry sun,
scratching at my pores. I miss this
place. It aches in my chest. What else?
Owls at night—not often heard, so
it's special. Elk bugling at dusk.
When the clock ticks that not a soul
is awake and the world bleeds deep
indigo. The color of this ink. Plunging
into an icy stream that shoots adrenaline
from my toes to my nose. The breath
I take is a good one. Not risky. Not
spending saved up somethings.
