The snake river has wound brown torrents through the valley again
Spring is swallowed whole by dull rain
The last cowboy arrives with spurs dug into green
But for the first time this year
Nighthawks sits on my desk like
A parallel scene in its stillness

I can lay on my bed forever now through the light rays and shadows
My bed is so good and so nice
I can burn leaves and watch them waft out the shutters

The blue ball-tip 0.7 pens are a pile in the corner and
I still wish I had a cat—a sage to grace this room

Jorts are in
I suck at writing poetry feed me
To the Wolves
