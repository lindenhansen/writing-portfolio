There is a particular kind of afternoon that arrives in late January, when the light is low and flat and the world seems to be holding its breath between two seasons.

I have been thinking about that light lately, and about the way certain moments resist being written down—not because they are too large, but because they are too exact. They belong to a specific hour, a specific temperature, a specific angle of the sun, and language can only approximate.

Still, we try. We have always tried. Maybe the attempt is the point, and the failure is not failure at all but something closer to reaching—the act of reaching toward a thing that keeps moving away.

I find comfort in that. In the trying.
